## Writing Guidelines

### Opening Paragraph:
**❌ Weak:** "I am writing to apply for the Software Engineer position I saw on LinkedIn."

**✅ Strong:** "I've been following [Company]'s journey in revolutionizing [industry] for the past two years, and I'm thrilled to apply for the Software Engineer position. As someone who built a similar [feature/product] that scaled to [X users], I'm eager to contribute to your mission of [company mission]."

### Body Paragraphs:
**❌ Weak:** "I have 5 years of experience in software development and I'm proficient in Java and Python."

**✅ Strong:** "In my current role at [Company], I architected a microservices platform that reduced API latency by 45% while serving 3 million users—challenges I understand are central to scaling [Target Company]'s infrastructure. This experience in high-traffic systems, combined with my expertise in Java and Spring Boot, positions me well to tackle the backend optimization goals outlined in your job description."

### Closing Paragraph:
**❌ Weak:** "Thank you for your consideration. I look forward to hearing from you."

**✅ Strong:** "I'm excited about the opportunity to bring my experience in [relevant area] to [Company] and contribute to [specific goal/initiative]. I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my background in [key skill] can help your team achieve [company objective]. Thank you for considering my application—I look forward to the possibility of contributing to [Company]'s continued success."

## Style Adaptation

If the candidate's writing style is available from their manual context, adapt the cover letter to match:

### Formal/Professional Style:
- Longer sentences, sophisticated vocabulary
- Structured, traditional format
- Focus on credentials and achievements
- Example: "I am particularly drawn to this opportunity because..."

### Conversational/Casual Style:
- Shorter, punchier sentences
- More relaxed vocabulary (while remaining professional)
- Personal anecdotes and enthusiasm
- Example: "What excites me most about this role is..."

### Technical/Analytical Style:
- Precise language, specific metrics
- Problem-solution structure
- Focus on technical achievements
- Example: "I optimized the system architecture by..."

### Creative/Storytelling Style:
- Narrative elements
- Vivid descriptions
- Show passion through stories
- Example: "When I first encountered [problem], I knew..."

## Important Notes

1. **Personalization is Key**: Reference specific company initiatives, products, or values. Show you've done your research.

2. **Avoid Clichés**: No "I'm a passionate self-starter" or "I'm a team player with excellent communication skills."

3. **Quantify When Possible**: Specific numbers make your achievements more credible and memorable.

4. **Match Their Language**: If the JD uses specific terms (e.g., "growth hacking", "customer obsession"), incorporate them naturally.

5. **Show, Don't Tell**: Instead of "I'm a strong leader," say "I led a team of 8 engineers to deliver..."

6. **Length**: Aim for 300-400 words (3-4 paragraphs). Respect the reader's time.

7. **Writing Style Adherence**: If the candidate's manual context shows a specific voice, mirror it. Otherwise, default to professional but warm.

8. **Company Research**: If knowledge base includes why they're interested in this company specifically, use that! Otherwise, research common facts about the company.

9. **Truthfulness**: Only use real experiences from the knowledge base. Don't fabricate.

10. **Human Voice**: Sound like a real person, not a template. Vary sentence structure, use contractions where appropriate, show genuine enthusiasm.

Now, write the personalized cover letter!
